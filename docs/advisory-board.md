# RadTempCF Advisory Board Formation

## Purpose

The Advisory Board provides strategic guidance and expertise to help shape the direction of the RadTempCF project. Board members represent diverse perspectives from radiology, technology, and healthcare domains.

## Board Structure

### Composition
The Advisory Board consists of 5-9 members representing:
- Academic radiology leadership
- Private practice radiology
- Healthcare informatics
- AI/ML in radiology
- Patient advocacy
- Medical education

### Member Categories

| Role | Quantity | Term |
|------|----------|------|
| Chair | 1 | 2 years |
| Vice Chair | 1 | 2 years |
| Members | 3-7 | 3 years |

### Eligibility Criteria

Board members should have:
- Demonstrated expertise in radiology reporting or healthcare informatics
- Active involvement in the radiology community
- Commitment to open-source principles
- Ability to attend quarterly meetings
- No conflicts of interest that would compromise objectivity

## Selection Process

### Nomination
1. **Self-nomination**: Open to any community member
2. **Peer nomination**: Existing members or contributors can nominate
3. **Invited**: Project maintainers may invite domain experts

### Review Criteria
- Relevant expertise and experience
- Past contributions to the project or community
- Diversity of perspective and background
- Commitment to the project's mission

### Selection Steps
1. Call for nominations (annually)
2. Review by current advisory board + maintainers
3. Vote by existing advisory board
4. Announcement of new members

## Responsibilities

### Board Members
- Attend quarterly advisory meetings
- Provide strategic input on project direction
- Review major feature proposals
- Mentor community contributors
- Represent the project at conferences (as able)

### Chair Responsibilities
- Lead advisory meetings
- Coordinate with project maintainers
- Set meeting agendas
- Represent advisory board at maintainer meetings

## Meetings

### Quarterly Meetings
- **Format**: Virtual (Zoom/Google Meet)
- **Duration**: 60-90 minutes
- **Schedule**: Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4

### Agenda Topics
1. Project progress review
2. Feature roadmap discussion
3. Community growth strategies
4. Technical challenges
5. Open discussion

### Minutes
- Published to project wiki
- Available to community members

## Term Limits

- Maximum 2 consecutive terms (6 years)
- One term cooling-off period before re-election
- Emeritus status available for past members

## Compensation

- Advisory board positions are voluntary
- Project may cover travel for in-person meetings
- Recognition in project documentation

## How to Apply

Interested candidates should submit:
1. Brief bio (200 words)
2. Relevant experience
3. Vision for the project
4. Two references
